Lumbini Center for Sustainability

A group of national and international scholars came together to proposes to establish a center of excellence --Lumbini Center for Sustainability (LCS)-- as a knowledge platform with the aim to promote an informed policy debate and provide pragmatic policy advice or dossiers for enhancing human well-being and climate resiliency of the central Himalayan community. The center will embody global and regional efforts of individuals, institutions, and innovations and aims to spread the message of universal peace propounded by Lord Buddha as foundation for achieving development in harmony with nature. Keeping sustainable development and human welfare at its core, it proposes to combine methods of natural sciences, social sciences, and humanities disciplines as integrative threads for exploring connected human-ecological system (CHES) to be analyzed and understood at spatial, temporal, and organizational scales. The concept of community resilience and well-being is considered holistically to include various key elements of sustainable development such as the food-energy-water nexus, natural disasters, environmental rehabilitation, bio-diversity conservation, ecosystem services, community health, resiliency and adaptation capacity building, indigenous knowledge, culture and values. To that end, the remarkable variability in connected human-ecological systems (CHES) along the river corridors of Nepal’s Himalayan range, linking the Tibetan plateau in the north and the Indian plain in the south, provides the ideal conditions to investigate causes and consequences of climatic changes in one of most vulnerable regions in the world.

  • VISION

    • Keeping sustainable development and human welfare at its core, the Lumbini Center for Sustainability (LCS) aims to be the best regional research hub to raise and deliberate bold and creative ideas that combines methods of natural sciences, social sciences, and humanities disciplines as an integrative dynamic social-ecological system.

  • MISSION

    • The mission of this center of excellence LCS is to create a platform that brings together collaborators both regionally and globally to advance the understanding of the linkages and feedback mechanisms between biophysical, social, and economic factors.

  • STRATEGIC GOAL

    • The strategic goal is to promote an informed policy debate and provide pragmatic policy prescriptions for enhancing the well-being and climate resiliency of the Himalayan community.

National Science Foundation funded Research Planning Trip (December 2014/January 2015)



An interdisciplinary team of UNM faculty, Dr. Alok Bohara, Dr. Mark Stone, and Dr. Joe Galewsky, and the three graduate students, went to Nepal and had a very successful NSF-sponsored planning trip (December 2014-January 2015). This trip covered a wide range of discussions with various stakeholders in Kathmandu and all across the Gandaki River Corridor (WWF, Dept. of M&D, NTNC, KU just to name a few). The team traveled to Jomsom, Kagbeni, Muktinath, Pokhara, Palpa, Bhairahawa, and Chitwan and landed in Kathmandu for the final reflection meeting. This pilot project represents an initial step in our team's long-term research goal to advance understanding of the linkages and feedback mechanisms between biophysical and social factors that influence vulnerability and resilience to climate change across interacting spatial, temporal, and organizational scales. We define social factor broadly to include issues such as livelihood, food security, wildlife human conflict, health, adaptive behavior, coping strategies, and the quality of life.

Workshop to Establish Lumbini Center for Sustainability



At the final leg of the trip to Lumbini, the UNM team and its Nepali collaborators participated in a workshop which was attended by close to fifty people which included 2 Vice-Chancellors, several campus chiefs, local government officials, local personalities and journalists. The workshop was interactive, deliberative, and participatory, which resulted in several valuable comments and feedback. The event was sponsored by the PNMFoundation and the Polytechnic Institute (PNMHI).

Several scholars from KTM came to the meeting. The main goal of this workshop was to plant a seed for an international research hub aptly named as the Lumbini Center for Sustainability. The goal of the LCS is to promote three basic activities -- research, academics, and capacity building. The noble cause of a center like this outside the valley was welcomed by everyone, including the two VCs (Lumbini Buddha University and Mid-West). The polytechnic institute PNMHI's new building will host the LCS, and an international guest house will also be built in due course. These activities will be evolving organically.
