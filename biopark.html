Our Projects

Bio-Park and Learning Center

Given the proximity of the aquatic systems (the Danda Riverfront) near its college campus, PNMF is dedicated in developing the concept of the bio-park learning center as a part of the PNMHI/LCS’s academic environment.

This may prove to be a valuable laboratory to promote research, conservation, recreation, and awareness. In research, for example, there are two possibilities: 1) development of a long-term ecological monitoring program, and 2) using the river’s aqua-systems and its wetland area to develop plots for medicinal plant extraction related research.

The PNMF strives to develop and implement community level change through developing multi-sector partnerships with residents, businesses, different academic institutions and local governments to recognize in the eco-park.
