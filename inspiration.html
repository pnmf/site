Inspiration

Pratiman Neema Memorial Foundation is a non-profit organization, which initially began as an organization dedicated to enhance the general welfare of the people and the communities in the district of Pyuthan, especially in memory of a then governor of the district, Pratiman Chand Bohara.

Born in a small village called Aglung, in the neighboring district of Gulmi, the late Pratiman Chand Bohara had a remarkable career. He participated as a freedom fighter in the democratic liberation movement of 1950 that successfully overthrew the autocratic Rana dynasty. As one of many unsung heroes of the movement of democratic struggles, he led a group of soldiers turned freedom fighters to free the district of Palpa in western Nepal, a stronghold of the regime, and defended the hilly district from the southern advancement. In addition to being a man with a military career and civil administration, he was passionate about socio-economic causes and local development. He engaged himself in various forms of grass-root driven development work that included road building, establishment of the first high school in the Pyuthan region, and always believed in decentralization, self-reliance, and community development through local resources and volunteerism. He saw fundamental flaws in overly centralized administrative system and regional neglect, especially of those who were poor and powerless. He expressed his far-reaching visionary ideas in the form of letters and diaries, which were discovered after 50 years. It was turned into a biography by PNMF. The seed of inspiration came from a small elementary school media class project done around 2001 at Mark Twain Elementary School (Albuquerque, New Mexico) by his youngest grandson (Sudeep Chand Bohara). This fourth grader on the local News 101 clip spontaneously recounted his grandfather's legacy and said how proud he was of his Nepali heritage.
